From Houseboy to Millionaire: Nigerian Man Narrates How He Made it from Wheelbarrow Business

- A Nigerian man identified as Chukwuma Ekeh has stunned social media with how he joined the millionaires club just from wheelbarrow business

- Chukwuma who had worked as a houseboy teamed up with a stranger to start up a wheelbarrow making and rental venture in Anambra

- They now boast of a workforce of 20 employees and have touched many lives

Success, they say, is when preparation meets opportunity. But it may be a different story for Chukwuma Ezeh.

The young man shared on Twitter how he became a millionaire out of his wheelbarrows. He had not planned for this as he worked as a houseboy in Delta.

The idea came from a stranger whom Ekeh met at a market. The stranger identified as Boniface later turned out to be his partner in the business.

Ekeh said they started their new venture with six wheelbarrows and later grew to a point where they could employ people to help them the budding business.

They now boast of more than twenty employees who help in the day to day running of the business.

The business helped them invest in other businesses with the money made from the wheelbarrow enterprise.

"We started small with six wheelbarrows. I owned five. Boniface owned one. We charged our employees. They paid KSh 57 per day for renting the wheelbarrows. It seems small but out of it, we have created wealth," Ekeh said.

His story inspired many who went ahead to celebrate Ekeh and his friend.

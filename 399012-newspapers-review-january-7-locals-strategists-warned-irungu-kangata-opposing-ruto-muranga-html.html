Kenyan newspapers review for January 7: Locals, strategists warned Irungu Kang'ata against opposing

The newspapers on Thursday, January 7, reported on the ongoing political discourse in the country with details about what transpired before Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata wrote the controversial letter to President Uhuru Kenyatta emerging.

The dailies have also reported on the challenges schools are struggling with in a bid to keep learners safe from COVID-19 infections.

1. Daily Nation

Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata hosted some 24 opinion leaders and political strategists at his Murang'a home on the night of December 31, 2020, to assess and analyse Mt Kenya politics.

During the meeting, the Senate majority chief whip is said to have asked his guests to be frank with him and tell him the truth about the reality on the ground.

The publication noted the outspoken senator was cautioned against being used to oppose and fight Deputy President William Ruto.

He was also asked to tell President Uhuru Kenyatta that Mt Kenya people were not happy with his perceived new political formation and were feeling betrayed and alienated

"He asked us to be honest with him because he wanted to be certain about the right political camp to join as he prepares to contest for Murang'a gubernatorial seat in 2022. The first thing we told him is that he was in a wrong camp and people were not happy with him since he was used to de-whipped William Ruto's allies," said Joshua Kigumo who was part of the meeting.

2. The Standard

Opposition chief Raila Odinga is said to be preparing to deal with the consequences of his handshake with Uhuru after sensing betrayal in the offing.

His recent move to disassociate himself with the failures of Jubilee government has been interpreted to mean he was still harbouring his dream to vie for the presidency.

Raila's latest letter that voiced support for striking doctors and health workers has also been highlighted as an indicator of what the ODM leader could be planning ahead of 2022.

He has also been silent about the BBI initiative for a while and pundits feel he has sensed mischief in the game

"It is a tactical retreat before we advance. I have not surrendered; we are advancing and I am certain we are going to succeed," said Raila.

3. People Daily

The government has ordered for 24 million COVID-19 vaccines which are set to arrive in the country before the end of the month.

At least 12 million Kenyans who are considered to be at a higher risk of being infected will receive the jab before the end of February 2021.

Health workers, teachers, police officers and the elderly have been put in the first category of individuals who will be vaccinated during the first round of the exercise.

4. Taifa Leo

The government has barred the media from accessing schools without permission from the Ministry of Education.

Analysts and policymakers, however, feel the government is gagging the media in order to cover up the mess in many schools that have failed to adhere to COVID-19 safety protocols as outlined by the Ministry of Health.

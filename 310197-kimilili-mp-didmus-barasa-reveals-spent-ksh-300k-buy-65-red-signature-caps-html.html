Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa reveals he has spent over KSh 300k to buy 65 red signature caps

Outspoken Kimilili MP Didmus has disclosed he imports his red signatures caps from Ghana at a cost of KSh 5, 000 each.

The lawmaker who is allied to Deputy President William Ruto's Tanga Tanga faction has so far bought 65 caps, making the total cost to stand at KSh 325, 000.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke reporter Kelvin Momanyi, the youthful politician said he feels comfortable whenever he puts on the red beret and his friends and electorates have come to identify him with it.

" Have 65 of these caps and I usually put them in my house and some in my car, even in my office you will find some. In case I forget putting it on before leaving the house I will still get one in my car. I have them in red, maroon and black,' he said

During his campaign, Barasa bought 20 pairs blue suits which he used as his identity and campaign tool.

"When I was campaigning my identity was a blue suit and people were wondering if this Jubilee aspirant had just one pair of the suit, but I had 20 of them," he revealed

Barasa is not strange to peculiar dressing code, he recently caused a stir online after he showed up in a public function wearing a patched short and walking bare feet.

The vibrant politician added drama to his outfit by donning a relatively large, old white shirt with a surprisingly long necktie depicting image of an old pupil.

Away from his attire, the Jubilee lawmaker is known for tongue lashing and political rhetoric which have established him as the champion of youth agenda, and defender of rights of the downtrodden.

The Ruto ally has also been making headlines for his rants that spare no one in the political sphere, particularly those opposed to DP's 2022 presidential bid.

"If you are government officer and you engage in planning how to assassinate the deputy president, we will deal with you perpendicularly," said in one of his past utterances

Outside the political field, Barasa is a cool and outgoing gentleman who freely interacts with locals and friends.

Governor Nyong'o urges Safaricom to build cancer centre, name it after Bob Collymore

Kisumu governor Peter Anyang' Nyong'o has appealed to Safaricom to build a cancer centre in honour of its late CEO Robert (Bob) Collymore who succumbed to leukemia on Monday, July 1.

The county chief suggested the oncology centre to be dedicated to Collymore should be set up in one of the leading local universities.

Nyong'o who was speaking to journalists in Kisumu on Tuesday, July 9, argued there were very few oncologists in Kenya despite increasing cases of cancer, thus necessitating the need to train more doctors specialised in treating and caring for cancer patients.

''We have few of these special doctors in the country. That is why a lot of time we have to seek for medication or specialised treatment abroad,'' said Nyong'o, himself a cancer warrior having been diagnosed with prostate cancer in July 2010.

The governor added once completed, the centre will be used to deliver expertise in cancer diagnosis, treatment and care including palliative care.

''Let it champion at every level of public healthcare cancer screening and diagnosis so that this 'Emperor of all Maladies' is identified early to make treatment more feasible," he said.

Nyong'o revealed Collymore was very instrumental in the operations of Africa Cancer Foundation (ACF), a Nairobi-based charitable trust launched in July 2011 to create awareness on the prevention, management and treatment of cancer in Africa.

The late Safaricom CEO was battling Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) and was in and out of hospital, including a trip to the United Kingdom (UK) in October 2017.

He continued with his treatment at various hospitals in Kenya even after his return from the UK.

His condition, however, worsened and he succumbed to the killer disease at his home in Nairobi.

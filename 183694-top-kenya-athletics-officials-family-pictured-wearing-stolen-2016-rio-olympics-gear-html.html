Keino's family trolled online after rumours allege they are wearing stolen Olympics gear

- Pictures of Kipchoge Keino daughter-in-law and her sons wearing Nike kits are making rounds on social media platforms

- It all started with a post on Facebook claiming that it was rather embarrassing for Sheila Komen to post photos of herself and her sons wearing oversized sports gear

- This comes after the Kenyan Olympics team suffered several setbacks in Rio including missing sports kits

Sheila Komen, daughter-in-law of the two-time gold medalist Kipchoge Keino did not know that posting innocent photos of herself and her sons on Facebook a couple of weeks ago would land her in hot soup.

Kenyans online are now having her for breakfast after rumour went around claiming that she and her sons were clad in sports kits meant for the Olympics sports team.

The photos of Komen and her sons dressed in Nike sports gear have gone viral on Facebook and WhatsAp. It is alleged that they are clad in the missing sports kits that were meant for the Kenyan team participating in the just concluded Rio Olympics.

No one has officially confirmed that the kits are those meant for the Kenyan team.

Keino is now in the middle of the Rio scandal where athletes failed to receive their Nike donations and suffered other ills under the National Olympic Committee of Kenya (NOCK).

On Saturday, August 27 police recovered hundreds of cartons containing Team Kenya uniforms that were meant to be used by the sports team at the Rio Olympics.

Several NOCK officials were arrested at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport after arriving from Rio De Janeiro, Brazil. They included the team chef Stephen Soi and NOCK chief executive officer James Chacha.

The committee's secretary Francis Paul was arrested at his home on Friday, August 26.

Keino threatened that Kenya could be banned from participating in Olympics if he gets arrested over the Rio scandal

Keino is one of the most celebrated Kenyan athletes and made history after he was awarded the first Olympic laurel by the International Olympic Committee in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

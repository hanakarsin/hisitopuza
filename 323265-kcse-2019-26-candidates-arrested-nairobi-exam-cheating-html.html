KCSE 2019: 26 candidates arrested in Nairobi over exam cheating

- The students were arrested at St. Teresa Boys Primary School and 35 mobile phones confiscated

- They were among 154 other private candidates sitting their 2019 KCSE examinations at the centre

- The students are said to have been receiving exam answers in real time via their mobile phones from unknown sources

- Officers administering the exams discovered the students were receiving answers during Chemistry Paper II exams

Police in Nairobi on Wednesday, November 6, arrested 26 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) candidates believed to have been involved in exams malpractice.

Starehe sub-county Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss Martin Mbaya said education officials also seized 35 mobile phones that were being used by the Form Four candidates.

A report by Daily Nation indicated the 26 candidates were arrested at St. Teresa Boys Primary School in Eastleigh where 154 private candidates were sitting their KCSE examinations.

“Owners of 26 of the mobile phones have been identified. Police are carrying out further investigations to identify owners of the nine other phones seized at the center,” said Mbaya.

He further noted that the officers administering the examinations discovered the students were receiving answers, real-time, from an unknown source during Chemistry Paper II exams.

It is now believed that the candidates were being fed with answers from people thought to be teachers.

“Investigations will continue to establish the source of the communications with the candidates and those behind the racket,” Mbaya added

According to Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha, those who were involved in cheating had photocopied the examination materials.

They then answered the questions and relayed back the answers to the candidates at the examination centre through mobile phones.

.“Most of those answers that are provided by teachers appear to be wrong. We must re-evaluate our morals as a society to end this practice of cheating in national exams,” said Magoha.

Education Principal Secretary Belio Kipsang and Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC ) acting chief executive officer Mercy Karogo later held a crisis meeting at the institution.

Magoha further warned the government will not relent in its efforts to have credible examinations.

The government had issued tough examination rules from the onset of the examinations, hoping to deter exam cheats, but it appears they cheats were determined to have their way.

The examination which started on Monday, November 4, will end on November 27, 2019.

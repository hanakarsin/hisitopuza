Music career

The rapper started his music career in 2005 and was fortunate to have an early breakthrough in the industry. He kicked off by posting a song on Soundclick in November that year, and it got positive feedback. This motivated him to start accounts on YouTube and Myspace, where he could post his jams.

In March 2007, he released his debut album, Unsigned & Still Major: Da Album Before da Album. One of the songs, Crank That, go its first airplay in May that year. It performed unexpectedly well and peaked at the first position on the US Billboard Hot 100.

It also peaked on Hot RingMasters charts and was later used on the television series, Entourage. Following the success of his debut single, he was signed with Interscope Records. In October that year, he released his first label album, Souljaboytellem.com.

Record Label

Besides making money from releasing and selling music, the rapper also owns and runs a record label called Stacks on Deck Entertainment (SODMG). He founded it in 2004.

Some of the artists the record has previously signed include A.Goff, Lil B, Killa Cam, John Boy, Tony The Deity, Shawty Boy, King Eazy, and Dae Dot. On 30th May 2016, the label announced it had dropped all its signed artists.

In 2019, the rapper announced that Lil 100 had been re-signed to the label. Today, the record label produces music by Lil 100, 24 hours, and Soulja Boy.

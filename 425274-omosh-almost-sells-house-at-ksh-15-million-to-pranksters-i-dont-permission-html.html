Omosh Almost Sells House at KSh 15 Million to Pranksters: &quot;I Don't Need Permission&quot;

  • Pranksters from Komic Gang caught Omosh unawares, ready to let go of his house for KSh 15 million
  • When approached at first, the former Tahidi High actor was adamant to sell the house but upon constant persuasions, he was ready to release it
  • The actor had initially vowed not to sell the house built for him by the Kikuyu town-based Sung Timbers in conjunction with other well-wishers

Former Tahidi High actor Omosh almost gave in to antics by comics who sought to buy his newly acquired house.

Posing as potential buyers, the pranksters arranged a meeting with Omosh, which he turned up without fail.

Upon meeting, they tried convincing him to sell the house, but he was adamant at first.

However, upon nagging by the "buyers", the one-time thespian was ready to exchange the house with the monies.

He demanded KSh 17 million from the pranksters who found the offer pricey.

Upon being enticed, he spoke highly of the house to woo the "buyers", stating that he was at liberty to sell. It was built for him by Sung Timbers in conjunction with other well-wishers.

After negotiating, the parties settled on KSh 15 million, which was to be deposited to Omosh's account immediately after the formalities are finalised.

Ultimately, he was baffled when it dawned on him that it had all been a game, the cast from Komic Gang being behind it.

House not for sale

While being handed the house two months ago, Omosh promised to take good care of it and that he would never sell it.

He had to clear the air following concerns from many Kenyans who believed he would sell the house to finance his alcoholic living.

Omosh was also echoing advice from most of the sponsors who saw the completion of his new house and who warned him against selling it.

To drive the point home, organisers of the handing over ceremony even had a dummy signpost written;

''This property is not for sale''. "'I was joking when I asked for help from Kenyans the second time. This poster reads this property is not for sale and for that reason I will never sell it. There are plots around my property that are up for sale, people can buy those but will never be selling mine,'' Omosh confirmed.

His declaration was received by ululations from his neighbours and everyone present during the handing over ceremony.

The latest development came about four months after Kenyans from different walks of life came out to help the actor.

Omosh, in an interview with Jalang'o pleaded for help from Kenyans holding he was in a financial crisis and couldn't even afford his house rent.

Embu: Youths miss out on KDF recruitment due to defective teeth caused by chewing miraa

- Many young people who had turned up at Embu Stadium for the KDF recruitment were rejected to defective teeth

- Lieutenant Colonel Philip Okoth, who was in charge of the exercise said the dental problems were caused by chewing miraa

- Okoth, however, lauded the exercise saying it was free and fair for all

Many youths' dreams to join the Kenya Defence Forces were cut short in Embu after they were found with a myriad of dental problems majorly due to chewing miraa.

Numerous young people who had turned up at Embu Stadium for the KDF recruitment were rejected to defective teeth such as cavities.

According to a report by Citizen TV, Lieutenant Colonel Philip Okoth, who was in charge of the exercise said though many youngsters had met many other qualifications, they could not be accepted into the force as the law requires recruits to be physically and mentally fit.

The colonel also said they had recorded the lowest number of recruits.

He said many youths missed out on the chance to join KDF due to lack of crucial academic certificates while others had crossed the age-limit.

Okoth, however, lauded the exercise saying it was free and fair for all further warning anyone soliciting or giving bribes will be dealt with according to the law.

The KDF will be conducting a month-long recruitment drive which began on February 8 to February 19.

In a paid-up advert on local dailies in January 2021, the KDF announced positions for the general service officer (GSO) cadets, specialist officers, general duty recruits, tradesmen and women.

The prospective candidates willing to join the KDF must be Kenyan citizen with no dual citizenship and be in possession of a valid national identity card.

Successful candidates for the above shall be notified through the print media between February 18 and February 28.

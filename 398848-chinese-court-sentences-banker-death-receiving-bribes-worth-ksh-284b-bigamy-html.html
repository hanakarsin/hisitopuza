Chinese court sentences ex-banker to death for receiving bribes worth KSh 28.4B, bigamy

- China's state broadcaster CCTV showed luxury cars and gold bands that Lai Xiaomin allegedly accepted as bribes

- The former banker served in the Chinese Central Bank and Chinese Banking Regulatory Board before his leading position in Huarong

- The sentence concludes one of the biggest cases of financial fraud in the country

- It comes with the increasingly tough approach Beijing takes towards corporate misconduct

China has sentenced the former president of China Huarong Asset Management Co. to death on charges of malfeasance, one of the most serious sentences arising from the anti-corruption campaign of President Xi Jinping.

Lai Xiaomin who was chairman of Huarong until he came under investigation in 2018, was sentenced to death for taking bribes and bigamy.

According to the court in Tianjin City, he was found guilty of collecting 1.79 billion yuan (KSh 28.4 billion) in bribes between 2008 and 2018, Yahoo News reported.

The court further noted it would confiscate all of his personal assets.

Last January, Xiaomin, a former member of the Communist Party, gave a thorough televised confession on state broadcaster CCTV.

The broadcast showed footage of cash-stuffed safes and cabinets in a Beijing apartment allegedly belonging to him.

During his TV confession, Xiaomin said he did not spend a single penny and instead just kept it there.

He had referred to the apartment where he kept the money as the "supermarket", given his regular visits there to deposit cash.

In trying to obtain the vast amount, the former banker had abused his power, a court in the northern town of Tianjin said, describing the bribes as "extremely large" and labelling the circumstances "particularly serious"

The court ruling added that he had shown extreme malicious intent.

The former chairman of the Hong Kong-listed China Huarong Asset Management Co. - a distressed debt company - was also found guilty of bigamy.

This was after he was found living with a woman as man and wife for long periods outside his marriage and fathering illegitimate children

Mo Shaoping, a lawyer based in Beijing, said it was unusual for bribery cases to result in the death penalty, with many ending up being reprieved from life in jail.

But in this case, Mo said the amount of corruption was particularly immense, probably the largest in recent years. The case caused public outrage as well.

A death sentence is undoubtedly sending a message in the current world — and most critically — breaking the illusion that cheating is not punishable by death.

Kamba MPs issue warning to Nairobi MCAs threatening to impeach Sonko

- MCAs have threatened to impeach Sonko for not appointing a deputy governor

- The city legislators are also annoyed by Sonko for running Nairobi from his home in Machakos

- Sonko appointed Miguna Miguna for the position but the embattled lawyer was rejected by MCAs

- He fronted four other prospective persons for the position but none of them has been approved for the job

Legislators from the Kamba community have warned that a plot by a section of Nairobi MCAs to impeach Governor Mike Sonko was destined for a great failure and disappointment.

TUKO.co.ke had reported that a scheme to kick Sonko out of office was brewing at city hall as the MCA's claimed the governor was running the county with hard hardheadedness.

Speaking during a memorial service of Sonko's late father in Machokos County, Makueni Senator Mutula Kilonzo Junior defended Sonko while stating that the motion to oust him will hit a snag immediately it was tabled in the Senate.

"I served with you in the previous Senate house and we made the laws governing impeachment. Dont let anyone scare you as they will not touch you. In the Senate, I am the Chief Whip and since I am good at understanding the law, their motion will die before it can proceed any further," stated Mutula.

The MCA's are said to have bad blood with the county boss who according to them, refused to appoint a deputy governor to help in the running of county affairs since Polycap Igathe, who served in the position, stormed out of city hall.

The MCAs also blamed Sonko for running the Nairobi from his rural home while he has offices assigned to him at the capital city.

Senator Mutula's sentiments were echoed by former Vice President Kalonzo Musyoka, who at the same time urged Sonko to move with speed and appoint an aide so as to forestall future clash with the MCAs.

" Give them a deputy governor. Just ensure you give them. I don't know what it means if you need to consult either the Jubilee and its party leadership. I understand that you gave them Miguna Miguna but they ended up rejecting him," stated Kalonzo.

On his part, Sonko dismissed any reports of impeachment terming them as a mere creation of the media.

He maintained that him and the city MCA's were inseparable and were reading from the same script.

"Myself and the city MCAs can differ on matters to do with opinion but we can never fight. We can not be divided and they (MCAs) will never arrive at a point where they want to impeach me even though the constitution allows them to impeach me if grossly violet my office," said Sonko as he claimed that he had not done anything to offend them.

Speaking at the same even, Ruai MCA John Kamangu showered Sonko with praises saying he was the only governor who understands how well to work with MCAs.

He watered down claims of splits emerging at city hall stating that all MCAs were moving in tandem with Sonko.

Since the Miguna was rejected by MCA's as a deputy governor nominee, Sonko has moved ahead and added to his list former Starehe MP Margaret Wanjiru, corporate guru Agnes Kagure, lawyers Karen Nyamu and Jane Weru but city hall is yet to settle on one.

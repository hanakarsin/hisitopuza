A political row is looming in the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) after KANU party leader Gideon Moi declared his 2022 presidential bid.

Amani National Coalition (ANC) boss Musalia Mudavadi, one of the OKA's principals, has maintained his firm stance on being the outfit’s 2022 presidential flag-bearer.

Staging a strong presidential campaign

But speaking on Friday, July 30, during a thanksgiving ceremony at Ruth Kitui Girls Secondary School in Baringo North, Moi, who is also the Baringo senator, promised to stage a well-oiled campaign for the top seat.

The senator made the declaration after Baringo North MP William Cheptumo challenged him to stop being lukewarm about his political ambitions.

''You know I don’t have to say the obvious. Unless he (Cheptumo) is a visitor in Jerusalem, so expect me on the presidential ballot in 2022,'' said Moi.

On Thursday, July 29, KANU secretary-general Nick Salat declared the Independence party would not be signing a deal with OKA principals anytime soon, exposing the intrigues roiling the fledgling outfit.

KANU's partnership with Jubilee

The Kanu official said the resolution that endorsed Kanu’s partnership with the Jubilee Party, as passed by the National Delegates Convention in 2017, is binding until next year.

“Right now, we don’t want to be coerced or forced into making a decision that would require the resolution of an National Delegates Convention,” Salat said.

Even as Salat spoke, Moi, on the other hand, pleaded with National Super Alliance (NASA) principals to unite and work together ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Addressing KANU delegates from Kirinyaga at Kabarnet Gardens, Nairobi, on Wednesday, July 28, the Baringo senator asked his fellow party leaders to join hands amid NASA coalition fallout.

"Musalia Mudavadi, Kalonzo Musyoka, Moses Wetang'ula and Raila Odinga, if we work together and support the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI), and inclusivity for all then as we push for the prosperity of the nation we must be together," he said.

Ford Kenya party exits NASA coalition

On Saturday, July 31, the Ford Kenya party's National Executive Committee (NEC) governing council ratified the official party's withdrawal from the NASA coalition.

NEC directed the party leadership to fast track engagement with other like-minded parties to form a coalition with the proposed approval of OKA.

List of 17 Senior Financial, Legal Experts Appointed by Uhuru to Clean Up Kenya Power

  • The team includes Bernard Ngumi, Nzioka Waita and State Department for Public Works PS, Major General Retired Gordon Kihalangwa
  • It will be mandated with the implementation of a consumer reduction from an average of KSh 24 kilowatt per hour to KSh 16
  • The government has suspended the purchase of power from all independent power producers

President Uhuru Kenyatta has appointed a 17-member inter-ministerial steering committee to report on power purchase agreements.

The team includes Bernard Ngumi, Nzioka Waita and State Department for Public Works PS, Major General Retired Gordon Kihalangwa.

Other members include Aaron Ringera, Mohammed Nyaoga, Elizabeth Muli, Stanley Kamau, Wanjiku Wakogi, James Mwangi, Anne Erikson, Mohammed Nyaoga, Yida Kemoli, Caroline Kittony, Sachen Gudka, Elizabeth Rogo, John Mutua and Ken Ogeto.

The mandate of the team

The team will implement a consumer reduction from an average of KSh 24 kilowatt per hour to KSh 16.

The committee will also streamline Kenya Power and turn around the utility firm in six months.

In a gazette notice, Uhuru extended the term of the Kenya Power taskforce.

The mandate of the 17-member team has been modified to include the implementation of its recommendation in a report presented to the president.

Special gov't project

Citizen TV reports that the government recently declared Kenya Power a special government project.

The first progress report is expected before December 5, 2021.

According to the Gazette notice, the committee will be mandated with overseeing, coordinating and monitoring the implementation of the recommendations of the presidential task force.

The priority is to lower the cost of power in the country ballooning due to the skewed power contracts between Kenya Power and independent power producers.

Power producers

Already, the government has suspended the purchase of power from all independent power producers.

According to the team's projections, Kenyans with the steering committee should expect power reduction within the first four months.

It is equally recommended that the overhaul of Kenya Power refit it into a proper commercial entity and demystifies the shadow power deals with independent power dealers.

Lawyer who represented DP Ruto in ICC to handle another Kenyan case

- Lawyer who led DP Ruto's defense team at the ICC to handle another Kenyan case

- The high profile case has been filed by Ruto's tribesmen, the Nandi

- The community has sued the British government for atrocities

A lawyer who represented Deputy President William Ruto during his trial at the International Criminal Court (ICC) in The Hague is about to begin handling another high profile Kenyan case.

Karim Khan who was the lead counsel during the DP’s trial will pocket KSh 108 million during the high profile case in which he will be representing Ruto’s tribesmen.

The Nandi have filed a case against the British government for the crimes committed by the British soldiers during the Nandi Resistance in 1905.

The Star reports that Khan’s law firm won the tender and that the Nandi county government has set aside KSh 108 million as legal fees.

Khan will now represent the community in the high profile case in London set to being late November 2016 and which is expected to rake in billions for the community in compensation should the Nandi win it.

During the trial, the Nandi people will be demanding an apology from the British government for the atrocities committed over 100 years ago.

The community will also seek compensation for the killing of over 10,000 people, rape and forceful evictions.

This includes the killing of the legendary Nandi leader Koitalel Arap Samoei in October 19,1905.

The Nandi will be relying on several such past cases including a recent case which resulted into the compensation of the Mau Mau fighters who were paid billions by the UK government, an apology and a monument at Uhuru Park’s Freedom Corner.

“We have several authorities at our disposal, including the indigenous people of Australia-the Aborigines-and the Mau Mau cases among others,” said Nandi governor Cleophas Lagat as quoted by the Star.

Koitalel was murdered during a peace meeting in Nandi Hills and more than 1.5 million heads of cattle were stolen when the local community was evicted.

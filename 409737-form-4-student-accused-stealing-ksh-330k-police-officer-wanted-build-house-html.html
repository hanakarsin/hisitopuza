Ngito Secondary School student has been arraigned over allegations of stealing money from a police officer.

Gideon Rotich, who was a candidate in the just concluded 2020 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education exams, was accused of withdrawing KSh 331,850 from Nicholas Koech's Police Sacco.

The prosecution team told Milimani Law Courts on Friday, April 23, that Rotich used a mobile number registered under his mother's name to effect the withdrawals between July and September 2020.

The victim, who was based in Garissa county at the time of the incident, said he discovered something was amiss when he visited an ATM booth to withdraw some cash.

The officer filed a complaint at the Sacco offices and requested a statement, after which he realised there had been transactions on diverse dates via a mobile money service.

The investigators traced the mobile phone number to Sarah Chelangat, Rotich's mother.

Chelangat admitted to the detectives that she had used her national identification details to register the mobile number on behalf of her son.

Elsewhere, a Form Four student at St George's Grassland Girls High School in Nakuru sneaked out of the school after being detained over fees arrears.

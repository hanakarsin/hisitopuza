Nelson Havi recalls being admitted as second last student at Musingu, exited with A minus

- Nelson Havi joined Musingu High School in 1992 and exited in 1995

- He was the second last student to be admitted and was relegated to the peasant stream A (Music and Art and Design)

- Four years later, he graduated with an A minus, being among the top three students

Law Society of Kenya (LSK) President Nelson Havi has shared memories of his high school journey at Musingu secondary where he graduated with an A minus after four years of toiling.

Havi, who visited the school to familiarise himself with the environment after more than 25 years of being away, recalled being the second last student to be admitted in Form One in 1992.

As a result of the late admission, the LSK president was relegated to the peasant stream A (Music and Art and Design).

He, however, managed to score an A minus and emerged among three top performers in the institution's candidate class of 1995.

"Enter Friends School Musingu High School in 1992 as the second last student in a class of 160 newly admitted students and relegated to the peasant stream A (Music and Art and Design). Exit in 1995 as one of the best three students with an A-," he recalled through his Twitter handle.

Legal proffession

Havi has risen through the ranks of the legal profession to become one of the most sought-after lawyers in the country.

He has been involved in a number of big and highly emotive cases involving corruption, land disputes, human right violations, electoral fraud among others.

The advocate has represented senior politicians, firms and business moguls in the country in various legal tussles.

He is the 49th president of the Law Society of Kenya and one of the youngest lawyers to occupy that office since its inception.

Controversy

The Musingu High alumnus has established himself as a bold and aggressive legal practitioner with an insatiable appetite to causing trouble and sparking controversy by making strong statements.

He is currently under siege from a section of LSK members who want him out over allegations of abuse of office and violation of laws and regulations guiding operations of the lawyers' body.

Some of his colleagues have accused him of highhandedness and a bossy attitude that has made it difficult for him to work with council members.

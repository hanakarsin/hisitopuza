Ladies, tell men about your past at your own risk, Sandra Dacha

- Generally, men judge women based on their past, and women judge men from their present

- The tricky bit about sharing this past, however, lies in what the one you opened up to does with the information especially after a disagreement

- Kenyan actress Sandra Dacha opines that women should keep their former escapades secret because men have a tendency to bring them back

Everyone has a past. Saints have a past, and sinners have a future. Men and women of God have a past. You too have a past. Which means every potential boyfriend or girlfriend has a past.

Now, the difference with how we handle people comes not in how good or bad the past was, but the individuals they are in the present.

Call girls have metamorphosed into amazing wives and mothers, and one-time hardcore criminals have taken to pulpits and preached in ways that got sinners saved.

Which begs the question; when it comes to a new relationship, what impact does the past have on the future, especially if it can be used controversially against the one who opens up?

Award winning Kenyan actress and media influencer Sandra Dacha insists that women should keep all their former escapades under wraps because men have a tendency to bring them back.

"Ladies, stop telling men about your past, they gonna either use it against you or take you through it. Let them continue from where they met you. If they want history, many universities offer the course," she wrote.

Her sentiments stirred emotions online, creating a gender divide that pit two varying opinions against each other.

The male folk were adamant that women should share their pasts upfront to safeguard them against knowing things from third parties and when it is too late, like news of her having children from a past relationship.

To them, keeping such secrets is dishonesty that has destroyed good relations for eons once the truth came out.

Ladies were however adamant that most men pretend to be okay with the past when the relationship is still blossoming, but start alluding to the same when a disagreement happens and use it to judge the woman.

According to them, they do not mind sharing what happened in the past because it is usually a way of showing not only openness but also proof that they turned a new leaf and are in for the ride or die, the past notwithstanding.

Wajir: New Governor Ahmed Mukhtar Reshuffles CECs as Ousted County Boss Vows to Stay Put

- The newly sworn in Wajir governor Ahmed Mukhtar reshuffled five CECs in his new administration

- The affected CECs include Ibrahim Abdi Mohammed, who was moved to head Finance department, Ismail Sheikh Issak to Health Services, and Hussein Sugow Gedi to Education

- The reshuffle is to take effect immediately, and the handovers will be effected by May 31

The newly sworn in Wajir governor Ahmed Ali Mukhtar has reshuffled the County Executive Committee Members (CECs) despite the ousted governor, Mohammed Abdi Mahmoud, vowing to stay put.

The move shows that Mukhtar has now taken charge as the new Wajir governor following the impeachment of his former boss.

And now he is rearranging his administration for better service delivery to the people of Wajir. The changes by Mukhtar affected five CECs.

Ibrahim Abdi Mohammed was moved to head the Finance docket from Education and Youth Affairs; Ismail Sheikh Issak from the Finance docket to Health Services; and Hussein Sugow Gedi from the Health docket to Education.

Yussuf Abdi Gedi took over Agriculture and Livestock docket from the ICT and Trade office; Ahmed Sharif Bulle took over the new ICT and Trade from the Agriculture and Livestock docket.

Lastly, Bare Abdulahi Idris was put in charge of the Finance and Audit docket exchanging positions with Abdullahi Mohammed Hassan who will now take charge of the Manufacturing Investment and Industrial Development docket.

The reshuffle will take effect immediately and handovers by May 31.

However, they have been claims by the impeached governor Mohammed Abdi Mahmoud that Mukhtar was acting against the law.

Police were deployed in different locations in Wajir town from the airport to the central business district and the county headquarters amid tension.

The anti-riot police officers were deployed to stop the ousted county boss Mahmoud from returning to the office.

TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported that Wajir deputy governor Mukhtar took over the running of the county affairs following his swearing-in as the new county boss.

Muktar took the oath of office to become the third governor in a ceremony attended by the local political leadership in the North Eastern county.

Muktar assumed the governor's office following the ouster of his boss Mohamud whose impeachment by the county assembly the Senate upheld on Monday, May 17.

The county assembly accused Mohamud of gross misconduct, abuse of office, corruption and other related charges.

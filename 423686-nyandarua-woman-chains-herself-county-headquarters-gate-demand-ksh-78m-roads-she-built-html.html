First Batch of Afghan Evacuees Highly Anticipated by Ugandans Land at Entebbe

  • Uganda's Foreign Affairs Minister Jeje Odongo said that 51 evacuees included women and juveniles
  • The US had requested the Ugandan government to temporarily host at-risk Afghans and nationals on transit
  • President Yoweri Museveni had agreed to grant refugee status to 2,000 people fleeing Afghanistan following a request by the US

The Ugandan government has confirmed the arrival of Afghans following the August 15 Taliban takeover of the war-torn Asian country.

In a statement on Wednesday, August 25, Foreign Affairs Minister Jeje Odongo said that 51 women and juvenile evacuees landed in Entebbe International Airport aboard a privately chartered plane.

The US had requested the Ugandan government to temporarily host at-risk Afghans and nationals on transit to the US and other destinations escaping the Taliban rule.

"The decision to host those in need is informed by the government of Uganda's consistent policy of receiving refugees and persons in distress as well as playing a responsible role in matters of international concern," Odongo stated.

She added that the first batch of about 500 Afghan refugees was expected on Monday, August 16, but they did not arrive.

"We expect to host them temporarily before they can be relocated elsewhere by the US government. It was request from US government to His Excellency. We started the preparation on Monday, August 16," Anyakun told journalists.

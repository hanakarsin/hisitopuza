Court withdraws order barring Philomena Mwilu from acting as chief justice

- Justice Otieno lifted the order barring Mwilu for 11 days, pending an inter-partes hearing at the Meru High Court

- The court had blocked Mwilu from serving as CJ and from also occupying the deputy chief justice position

- She had also been inhibited from sitting on the Supreme Court bench as well as serving as a member of the Judicial Service Commission

The High Court of Kenya has withdrawn an order that barred Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu to hold the office of the chief justice in an acting capacity.

The court had earlier blocked Mwilu from serving as CJ and from also occupying the deputy chief justice position pending the determination of a case filed by city lawyer Isaiah Mwongela.

On Monday, February 1, Justice Otieno lifted the order for 11 days, pending an inter-partes hearing at the Meru High Court.

The decision by justice Otieno to bar the DCJ from sitting in the JSC meant she would also not participate in the process of selecting the retired chief justice David Maraga's successor which is expected to begin after applications close on February 10.

She had also been inhibited from sitting on the Supreme Court bench as well as serving as a member of the Judicial Service Commission in the Friday, January 29, ruling.

In his petition, the city lawyer stated that he had adequate grounds for bringing criminal proceedings against justice hence her unfitness for office.

Mwongela further stated that the positions of Justice Mwilu as a member of the JSC and the Ombudsperson of the Judiciary could undermine her prosecution as she plays a critical role in disciplining members of the Judiciary.

Mwilu ascended to the seat of the chief justice after Maraga went for retirement in January 2021.

As TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported, Activist Okiya Omtatah had also moved to court to block Mwilu from taking after Maraga.

In the petition, the activist argued Philomena should not succeed Maraga because of the corruption allegations levelled against her by the Director of Public Prosecution (DPP).

Omtatah said the deputy DJ should only be allowed to ascend to the seat of Chief Justice if the graft accusations are cleared.

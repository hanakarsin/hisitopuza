Newly Elected FORD Kenya MP Kalasinga Takes Ruto's Greetings to Kabuchai Residents

  • Kalasinga, who was in Chwele ward over the weekend delivered greetings from his party leader Senator Moses Wetang'ula and Deputy President Ruto
  • The newly-elected legislator's remarks pointed to a possible switch to Ruto's United Democratic Alliance
  • Kalasinga contested for the Kabuchai seat on the Jubilee Party ticket in the 2017 and 2017 general elections, which he lost

The newly elected Kabuchai MP Majimbo Kalasinga could be on his way to the Ford Kenya, which sponsored him to the National Assembly.

Based on remarks he made recently in the Kabuchai constituency, all indications pointed to a possible move to Deputy President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance.

In a surprise turn of events, the politician who was in Chwele ward over the weekend delivered greetings from his party leader Senator Moses Wetang'ula and Speaker Ken Lusaka.

The former Jubilee politician also disclosed he had been sent to deliver similar messages from Ruto, a move that left Kabuchai political observers with unanswered questions.

"Our leader senator Moses Wetang'ula asked me to greet you people of Chwele, have you received the greetings? I have also brought greetings from Speaker Ken Lusaka, have you received them? And greetings from DR William Samoei Ruto...those are the only things I was asked to say when I come here," he stated.

Kalasinga garnered 19,274 votes, representing 65% of the total votes cast, while his closest rival, Evans Kakai of the United Democratic Alliance, was second with 6,455 votes representing 22%.

Kalasinga first contested for Kabuchai parliamentary seat in 2013 on a Jubilee ticket but came in a distant third, behind winner James Lusweti and Philip Mukui ( both deceased).

In 2017, he contested for the seat again on the Jubilee Party ticket but, like in 2013, lost and emerged third, behind Lusweti and independent candidate Tale Nabangi (deceased).

The politician was successful after he decamped to Ford Kenya, which draws its roots and strength from Bungoma county and parts of Trans Nzoia.

The seat fell vacant following Lusweti's death on December 4, 2020.

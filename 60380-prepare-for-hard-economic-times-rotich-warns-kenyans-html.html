Treasury Boss Warns Of Tough Economic Times Ahead

The holder of the country's purse has gone on record asking Kenyans to prepare for difficult economic times as the National Treasury develops a recovery strategy. 

National Treasury Cabinet Secretary Henry Rotich on Sunday November 1 reportedly told Kenyans to "tighten their belts" as the country was negotiating an economic turnaround strategy.

Rotich likened the current economic mess to a situation where passengers flying at an altitude of 40,000 feet are hit by turbulence and their only hopes are in the pilots.

He was speaking in Eldoret.

But while Rotich warned Kenyans on this, the head of State took to the podium to assure Kenyans that the economy is strong and there was no cause for alarm.

President Uhuru Kenyatta said Kenya's economy was "okay" in a statement that sought to end anxiety among citizens.

The talk about economic crisis gripping the country, he noted, was a creation of the opposition in Kenya.

But despite the assurances, there are reports of bank interest rates shooting through the roof, the government borrowing money from commercial banks at the exorbitant rates, a weak Kenyan shilling compared to international currencies and Kenya Revenue Authority falling short of its collection targets.

Parliament has also passed a law to increase taxes in certain products including soft drinks, bottled water, cigarettes and imported cars as a part of measures to jolt the economy reportedly on its knees.
